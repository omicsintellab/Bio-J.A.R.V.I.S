This virus is approximately 9460 nucleotides in size and is the etiological agent responsible for Hepatitis. HCV is transmitted through blood and sexual contact and primarily infects humans. The virus has been extensively studied due to its significant impact on human health, with a well-defined taxonomic classification within the Flaviviridae family. The clinical implications of HCV infection are profound, leading to chronic liver disease, cirrhosis, and hepatocellular carcinoma, underscoring the importance of accurate identification and monitoring of this pathogen in clinical settings.
